Millard 2017 — ATP drain (6-panel)

Steady-state baseline (t=0–500s, red-dashed line) → ATP dropped to 30% of steady state (mass-balanced into ADP) → recovery transient. The Millard model recovers ATP within ~50s — glycolysis (G6P/F6P/FDP transients in upper-mid) and oxidative phosphorylation (FAD/Q transients lower-right) co-respond. Energy charge (dashed black line in upper-left, right axis) drops to ~0.5 at perturbation and snaps back. This experiment exercises every coupled subsystem in the central-metabolism ODE.

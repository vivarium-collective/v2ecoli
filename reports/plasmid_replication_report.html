Mathematical model

RNA I / II / hybrid ODE (Ataai-Shuler 1986)

\[ \begin{aligned} \frac{d[\text{RNA}_{\text{I}}]}{dt} &= K_T^{\text{I}} \cdot N_{\text{plasmids}} - k_h \cdot [\text{RNA}_{\text{I}}][\text{RNA}_{\text{II}}] - \gamma_I\,[\text{RNA}_{\text{I}}] \\ \frac{d[\text{RNA}_{\text{II}}]}{dt} &= K_T^{\text{II}} \cdot N_{\text{plasmids}} - k_h \cdot [\text{RNA}_{\text{I}}][\text{RNA}_{\text{II}}] - \gamma_{II}\,[\text{RNA}_{\text{II}}] \\ \frac{d[\text{Hybrid}]}{dt} &= k_h \cdot [\text{RNA}_{\text{I}}][\text{RNA}_{\text{II}}] - \gamma_H\,[\text{Hybrid}] \end{aligned} \]

Initiation criterion

\[ \Delta\,\text{PL}_{\text{fractional}} \;=\; N_{\text{plasmids}} \cdot f \cdot \exp(-k_h \cdot [\text{RNA}_{\text{I}}] \cdot t_{\text{tx}}) \]

Every 1 / K_T^II ≈ 360 s, each plasmid fires one RNA II initiation attempt. The surviving fraction (not bound by RNA I during transcription time t_tx) is multiplied by primer efficiency f and added to the fractional accumulator. When the accumulator crosses 1.0, one integer initiation fires.

Elongation (polymerize algorithm)

\[ \text{sequences} = \text{buildSequences}(\text{template}, \; \text{fork}_{\text{pos}}, \; v \cdot \Delta t) \quad\Rightarrow\quad \text{result} = \text{polymerize}(\text{sequences}, \; [\text{dNTP}], \; R_{\text{limit}}) \]

Unidirectional (one replichore, 2 sequence rows). Mass increase per fork: Δm = Σ nnt · wnt (fg). One PPi released per dNTP polymerized.

Termination

\[ \text{coord} \ge L_{\text{replichore}} \;\Rightarrow\; \text{delete replisome, create new full\_plasmid} \]

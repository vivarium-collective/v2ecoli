Implication

Compare the truth-identifiability ratios above. If the count-based metric gives a much higher truth-vs-next-nearest ratio than the log-likelihood-based one, the self-calibration hypothesis (sprint 11) is confirmed and the architectural answer is "for ABC, distance on count observables, not on emitted log-likelihoods". If they're similar, the trajectory-level RNG variance dominates both metrics and the choice is less critical.

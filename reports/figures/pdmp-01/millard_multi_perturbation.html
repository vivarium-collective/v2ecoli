Millard 2017 - three side-by-side perturbation experiments

Three qualitatively distinct stress experiments, all from the same steady-state initial conditions, perturbed at t=500s (vertical dashed line), recovery traced to t=3000s. Same axes for direct comparison.

  • ATP→30% (red): energy drain. Tests glycolytic + ox-phos recovery.
  • PEP→10% (amber): glycolysis-bottleneck. Tests upper-glycolysis ramp-up to refill PEP.
  • NADH→5x (blue): redox overload. Tests oxidative-phosphorylation throughput + TCA back-pressure.

The trajectories show three qualitatively different responses on the same observables: each panel reads as a "fingerprint" of the perturbation. ATP and NADH recover quickly across all experiments (~50s) due to the model's tight coupling; PEP and MAL show slower transients indicating propagation through glycolysis and the TCA cycle.

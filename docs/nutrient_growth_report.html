Why does growth continue after [GLC]ₑₓₜ hits zero? Three reasons stacked:
  1. ~17 molecules have unconstrained exchange bounds (AMMONIUM, OXYGEN-MOLECULE, Pi, SULFATE, WATER, K⁺, MG²⁺, NA⁺, Cl⁻, Fe²⁺/³⁺, Mn, Zn, Ca, Co, Ni, selenocysteine, CO₂). Once MM caps glucose uptake at 0, the LP keeps pulling on the others.
  2. The homeostatic objective pins internal metabolite concentrations toward fixed targets (set by getBiomassAsConcentrations). As the cell grows, these targets rescale — the LP keeps producing internal metabolites even when their upstream carbon supply is off.
  3. No carbon-balance constraint on biomass. The biomass reaction's coefficients assume carbon comes from somewhere; with GLC blocked the LP finds carbon in the CO₂ fixation / reverse-TCA routes that have catalysts present, at reduced efficiency but non-zero flux.
A proper exp→stat transition needs at least one of: a carbon-balanced biomass objective (no carbon source → no biomass), a ppGpp-coupled biomass downscaling, or hard flux bounds on the "promiscuous" inputs above when their carbon-equivalent contribution would violate a mass balance.

What this report does not cover yet

  • Non-glucose carbon sources: glycerol, gluconate, lactate. Requires media recipes + per-carbon v_max/K_m.
  • Ion stress: Na⁺, Mg²⁺ gradients from Caglar's figure 2B/C.
  • ppGpp-driven transition: mechanistic stationary- phase entry via the existing ppgpp_initiation pathway.

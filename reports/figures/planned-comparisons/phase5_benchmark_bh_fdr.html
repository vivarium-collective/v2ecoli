For each benchmark E. coli gene with a known function, the Phase 5 pipeline runs a Bayesian model comparison (Russian Roulette marginal-likelihood estimator, Lyne et al. 2015) between the true annotation and each candidate alternative. The raw p-value derived from the Bayes factor is BH-adjusted across the gene-set (controlling FDR at q ≤ 0.10). Genes whose observed p is below the red BH threshold line are declared "successfully recovered." Success criterion for the investigation: ≥80% of the 20-gene benchmark recovers at q ≤ 0.10, with credible-interval widths shrinking at the ≥n^(-1/2) rate as sample size grows.

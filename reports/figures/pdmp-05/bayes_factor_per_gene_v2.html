Per-gene Bayes factor

methodology phase5-output

For each candidate gene, the Bayes factor K compares H1 (gene is a causal driver of growth) to H0 (gene is independent). K > 10 = strong evidence; K > 100 = decisive. With per-gene BH FDR correction at α=0.05, Phase 5 produces a calibrated discovery list — far more rigorous than the typical fold-change cutoff used in transcriptomics.

TL;DR

  • fold_changes_removed.tsv is a list of 36 (TF, Target) curation-override rows the Covert lab introduced to filter specific edges out of fold_changes_nca.tsv at ParCa knowledge-base-load time.
  • An empirical with/without comparison shows ~12 of the 36 workarounds are load-bearing — removing them drops the target 2-5× below experimental. AA-biosynthesis enzymes (serA, glnA, aspC, can, ilvA/D/E, pyrI, gltX) take the biggest hit; ribosomal proteins (rplI, rpsF, rpsR) drop ~2×. Ribosomal-protein totals sit 2-8× above experimental in both states — a global calibration issue independent of workarounds.
  • The v2ecoli Phase 1-3 LexA-routing fix (d5788d6) replaces only the lexA→rpsU workaround with a structural alternative (dedup-exemption + per-promoter ratios + TF-TU routing). The other 35 remain load-bearing.
  • Each load-bearing workaround is a candidate for the same kind of structural treatment — analyse the operon, identify the dedup-misrouting pattern (if any), and design a per-operon fix.
  • serA fix applied + ppGpp NNLS-degeneracy patched (2026-05-24) — dedup was keeping the weak TU (serAp2) and dropping the dominant one (serAp1, ~97% of basal mRNA per Thomason 2015). 3 TSV edits route TFs to the correct TU + add Thomason ratios. Diagnostic revealed a second bug: set_ppgpp_expression runs NNLS on exp_free / exp_ppgpp directly, which is degenerate for dedup-exempt operons (zeros out non-survivor TUs after the basal ratios were applied earlier). A 2-line re-application of _apply_per_promoter_ratios after the NNLS step fixes it. Net result: serA total protein 142 → 13,940 copies/cell vs Schmidt 11,353. Other proteins unchanged. See Case study.

What fold_changes_removed.tsv does

Flat file at reconstruction/ecoli/flat/fold_changes_removed.tsv (vEcoli) and v2ecoli/processes/parca/reconstruction/ecoli/flat/fold_changes_removed.tsv (v2ecoli). Each row is (TF, Target, _comments). The KB loader matches each row's (TF, Target) tuple and removes any matching row from both fold_changes.tsv (literature-curated) and fold_changes_nca.tsv (NCA-derived). This happens during KnowledgeBaseEcoli._prune_data() before any downstream pipeline step runs.

The intent in each comment is consistent — keep a particular gene's basal expression in a usable range despite known cross-talk artefacts (ppGpp+LexA over-repressing ribosomal subunits, mechanistic-supply destabilising amino-acid biosynthesis, etc.).

Reading the landscape:
  • Ribosomal proteins (rpsU, rplI, rplR, rpsR, rpsF) are 2-8× over experimental in both states — that's a global ribosome-allocation calibration issue, not a workaround issue, and isn't addressable by the dedup-fix pattern.
  • AA-biosynthesis enzymes (serA, glnA, aspC, can, ilvD, ilvE, pyrI, ilvA, gltX) sit within ~2× of experimental with workarounds; stripping them drops the targets 2-5× below experimental. That's the real load-bearing role of those workarounds.
  • leuA and gdhA move up when stripped — those workarounds are feedback-loop breaks (leuO→leuA, argP→gdhA), removing them lets the activator chain run.

How I will look at the candidates

The rpsU fix (d5788d6 + ddf4970) sets the template. For each load-bearing workaround, do the same three-step analysis:

  1. Operon structure — identify which TUs the Target sits on; check whether ParCa's dedup-and-pool step is collapsing the operon onto a single surviving TU; identify which promoter survives (housekeeping vs. regulated).
  2. Regulator routing — trace where the TF's curated FC actually lands after dedup. If the FC is routed to the housekeeping promoter instead of the regulated one, the workaround is masking a misrouting bug (same pattern as rpsU).
  3. Structural alternative or accept — if misrouting, design a per-operon fix (dedup exemption + per-promoter ratios + TF-TU routing entry). If the workaround addresses something other than dedup misrouting (e.g. feedback loop, rRNA regulation), document why a structural fix isn't applicable and leave the workaround in.

Triage order

Process candidates by impact (collapse magnitude × biological centrality):

  1. rplI next — 1300× collapse in v2ecoli; large-subunit ribosomal protein, part of rplJL–rpoBC operon. Most likely same nested-promoter pattern as rpsU.
  2. rpsR / rpsF queued — both small-subunit ribosomal proteins, 2× drop. Different operons. Check whether nested promoters apply.
  3. gltX queued — Glu-tRNA synthetase, 4× drop. fis→gltX — single-TF case. Check whether fis binds a specific TU only.
  4. rpsO queued — removing the three TF workarounds (argR/crp/fis) increases the count. Different pattern; may be over-counting via multiple promoter contributions.
  5. leuA low priority — modest effect; leuO→leuA is a stated feedback-loop break, likely structurally irreducible.
  6. Amino-acid biosynthesis group (ilvA/D/E, glnA, aspC, can) skip in basal — all ~0 in basal in both pipelines. Workarounds only matter in +AA conditions, out of scope here. (serA and pyrI handled below: serA is rescued; pyrI is a separate, non-dedup issue — see Open questions.)
  7. rRNA group (fis/hns/lrp → rr*) skip — rRNA regulation is a separate machinery, unlikely to share the dedup-misrouting pattern.

Per-candidate deliverable

For each candidate, produce one short note covering: operon TU layout, the TF's curated FC, where it lands post-dedup, whether the misrouting pattern applies, and either (a) a draft Phase-1-3-style structural fix or (b) a justification for keeping the workaround.

Case study — serA (Lrp-repressed, 2 nested promoters)

Biology (EcoCyc + Thomason 2015): serA has two σ⁷⁰ promoters — serAp1 (proximal, TSS at 3058456) and serAp2 (distal, TSS at 3058549). Both contain only serA (single-gene operons sharing the same gene_tuple). Per Thomason 2015 M63_0.4 dRNA-seq stepHeights (Thomason b2913 positions corrected by +~2000 bp offset to match v2ecoli's annotation):

TU00224serAp1 (proximal)103.95 (Primary)0.974Nac (repress, 2 sites); Lrp-apo (activate, shared site)
TU0-2342serAp2 (distal)2.840.026CRP-cAMP (activate); Lrp-apo (activate, overlaps TSS); Lrp-Leu (repress)

Problem: ParCa's gene_tuple-keyed dedup keeps only one TU per (sorted) gene tuple. The default cistron-to-TU routing then sent Lrp's effect to the wrong dominant TU.

What the fix accomplishes:
  • Both serA TUs are now distinct in rna_data
  • Per-TU mRNA distribution now matches Thomason (97% serAp1, 3% serAp2 in basal)
  • Lrp's NCA-derived repression FC now routed to serAp2 only (biologically correct per EcoCyc Lrp-Leu mechanism)
  • Downstream Lrp-perturbation analyses now reflect TF activity (not zero)
Why the 2-line transcription.py fix was needed: the 3 TSV edits alone were not enough. set_ppgpp_expression runs NNLS on the per-cistron exp_free / exp_ppgpp arrays separately from the basal-rates pipeline. For operons listed in per_promoter_ratios.tsv the NNLS is rank-deficient: it dumps the entire per-cistron mass onto a single TU (typically the dedup survivor) and zeros the rest. Re-calling _apply_per_promoter_ratios right after the NNLS step re-apportions per the biology-validated ratios.

Side finding: NCA sign error for Lrp

NCA gives lrp → serA = −2.44 log2 (repression), but per EcoCyc Lrp ACTIVATES serA at both promoters as Lrp-apo (no leucine bound) and only represses as Lrp-Leu. v2ecoli's TF model has one sign per (TF, target) pair, so it can't represent the dual mode. The fix routes the existing (repression) FC to serAp2 only — biologically reasonable since Lrp-Leu does repress there — while leaving serAp1 unregulated (the missing activator FC isn't in any of v2ecoli's data sources). The Covert workaround silenced the wrong-sign NCA value; we've replaced "silence everything" with "route correctly where the sign IS right".

Biology (EcoCyc + Thomason 2015): gltX (Glu-tRNA synthetase, b2400) is on the negative strand and has three σ⁷⁰ promoters within a 54 bp window. Per Thomason 2015 M63_0.4 dRNA-seq stepHeights (b2400 coords +1977 offset → v2ecoli):

Problem: ParCa's gene_tuple dedup keeps only one TU per (sorted) gene tuple. For gltX all three TUs share (EG10407,), so dedup keeps TU0-6409 — which, coincidentally, is gltXp3 (the dominant 95% TU where Fis actually binds). So default routing already lands Fis's fold-change on the biologically-correct TU. There's no dedup-misrouting to fix.

The workaround was masking this magnitude error.

Side finding: NCA silently overwrites curated

The merge loop in v2ecoli/processes/parca/reconstruction/ecoli/simulation_data.py (~line 156) iterates ["fold_changes", "fold_changes_nca"] in that order and blindly assigns tf_to_fold_change[tf][target] = 2**FC in each pass. The NCA pass therefore overwrites any curated entry for the same (TF, target). For gltX, NCA −1.93 won over curated −0.19. This is probably accidental — fixing the iteration order or adding an if (tf, target) not in self.tf_to_fold_change guard would make curated take precedence as one would expect. Tracked in Open questions below.

Case study — leuA (operon-wide LeuO, dedup-exempt routing semantics)

Biology (EcoCyc + Thomason 2015): leuA (α-isopropylmalate synthase, b0074) is the second cistron of the polycistronic leuLABCD operon on the negative strand. The operon is driven by a single dominant promoter (leuLp1, Thomason TSS at 83735, UTR 27) and a minor distal TSS (leuLp2, Thomason 83894, UTR 186). LeuO binds only leuLp1 and activates the entire transcript.

v2ecoli TUEcoCyc promoterThomason TSSUTRM63 stepHeightBasal fractionTF
TU00179leuLp1 (proximal)8373527981.52~0.99LeuO (activates)

Problem: dedup keeps TU00179 (alphanumerically first; biologically correct, since leuLp1 is the dominant primary). Default routing already lands every leuO → leu* FC on it. So the situation looks identical to gltX — routing is correct by accident. But the workaround for leuO → leuA in fold_changes_removed.tsv (note: only the leuA edge; leuO → leuL/B/C/D remain active) still leaves the operon over-expressed (~5× over Schmidt) and removing it makes things worse (~9×). The runaway from sentinel hints that LeuO regulation in the model is amplifying via NCA — but no curated value exists to fall back on for the gltX-style magnitude fix.

The real twist surfaced after applying the dedup-exempt structural fix: with per_promoter_ratios.tsv entries for the leu operon, the default-routing semantics for any leuO → leu* FC without an explicit tf_tu_routing.tsv entry change. Routing only leuO → leuA → TU00179 while leaving leuO → leuL/B/C/D as defaults made leuA jump 5× (to 36,662). Routing all 5 cistrons (leuL, A, B, C, D) explicitly to TU00179 restored coherent operon-level fitting, and the result — leuA = 2,394 — matches Schmidt 2,711 within 12%. After this, the fold_changes_removed workaround becomes unnecessary.

Side finding: polycistron operon-wide TFs need all-cistron routing

For an operon where a single TF binds a single promoter and activates the whole transcript, NCA produces a separate (TF, cistron) FC for every cistron (consistent magnitudes). When dedup keeps a single TU, default routing sends them all to that TU and the model treats the operon coherently. But once dedup is exempted, the default routing of any unrouted FC changes — routing only one cistron explicitly is worse than routing none, because the per-cistron expression then becomes inconsistent across the polycistron. Rule: for an operon-wide TF, route every cistron-level FC (not just the named target) to the same TU. Adds rows but keeps the fitting coherent.

  • can is a wrong-sign NCA workaround, not a dedup case won't-fix from this audit — can (yadF, EG12319, carbonic anhydrase 2) has 2 v2ecoli TUs (TU0-45255, TU0-8774) with identical coords (142008–142703) and identical single-cistron gene_tuple — they're redundant computational annotations (both EV-COMP-AINF-SINGLE-DIRECTON), not biological alternatives. Per EcoCyc, Lrp activates can — the NCA sign is wrong. The current "silenced" state is therefore closer to truth than restoring would be. To represent lrp → can correctly would need a curated positive FC in fold_changes.tsv (no measured magnitude readily available; placeholder would be required).
  • NCA sign-error pattern — emerging cross-candidate finding. Several load-bearing workarounds in fold_changes_removed.tsv mask NCA values whose sign disagrees with EcoCyc's curated direction, rather than (or in addition to) dedup-misrouting: lrp → serA = −2.44 vs EcoCyc Lrp-apo activates serAp1 (dual-mode); lrp → pyrI = −0.85 vs EcoCyc Lrp activates pyrI; lrp → can = −0.92 vs EcoCyc Lrp activates can; crp → glnA = −0.33 vs EcoCyc CRP dual-mode (act glnAp1, rep glnAp2). The Lrp cluster in particular suggests NCA mis-estimates Lrp-target signs systematically (likely because NCA can't distinguish Lrp-apo activation from Lrp-Leu repression — the in-vivo measurement is dominated by whichever mode is active in the proteome used to fit NCA). v2ecoli's schema (one sign per (TF, target)) can't represent the dual modes, so the workaround-removal approach is correct for those — but several of the Lrp workarounds may correspond to single-mode activators where the NCA sign is simply wrong. Would be useful to systematically compare every NCA FC sign against EcoCyc's curated direction before designing further fixes — distinguishes "needs routing" from "needs sign correction" from "needs schema extension."

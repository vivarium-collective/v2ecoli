F-02-gil-bottleneck colonies-01-hpc-readiness novel computational The GIL is the dominant bottleneck at colony scale.
full statement
The GIL is the dominant bottleneck at colony scale. Process-bigraph
composites are single-threaded by design — one process uses ~1 CPU core
regardless of how many cells it holds. Scaling means more processes, not
more cells/process.
F-06-rss-growth-over-time colonies-01-hpc-readiness novel computational RSS grows continuously during a single long N=1 run at ~5 MB/sim-second (1126 MB at tick 0 → 7016 MB by tick 1050, before division).
full statement
RSS grows continuously during a single long N=1 run at ~5 MB/sim-second
(1126 MB at tick 0 → 7016 MB by tick 1050, before division). Indicates
either accumulating state in the EcoliWCM internal composite or a leak;
visible only in long runs, not in the 60s N-sweep windows.

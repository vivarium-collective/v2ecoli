This PR implements the Phase 1 architectural seam from the DUF final report (Sec. 4.1.2): replacing v2ecoli's multi-objective FBA metabolism — the kFBA implementation (v2ecoli/processes/metabolism.py → wholecell.utils.modular_fba.FluxBalanceAnalysis, GLPK simplex, biomass + homeostatic + kinetic-constraint objectives) — with a piecewise-deterministic Markov process (PDMP) whose deterministic core is the Millard et al. 2017 kinetic ODE for E. coli central carbon and energy metabolism. The Phase 1 milestone — the WCM runs end-to-end with the kinetic-ODE substitution — is achieved. Performance and biology calibration are tracked as named follow-up tasks.

Terminology note. The DUF report (and the broader literature) often uses "tFBA" for v2ecoli's metabolism, after Birch et al. 2014 ("time-linked FBA": a sequence of LPs tracking target trajectories). The actual v2ecoli implementation is structurally different — a single multi-objective LP per timestep, not a target-trajectory sequence — so this investigation tracks it as kFBA (kinetic-constraint-augmented FBA). See the glossary in investigation.yaml.

1. Architecture — Before vs After

v2ecoli's actual metabolism — what this investigation calls kFBA — is a single multi-objective LP solved each timestep by wholecell.utils.modular_fba.FluxBalanceAnalysis (GLPK simplex) with three coupled objectives: biomass production (max cTv), homeostatic concentration targets (media + doubling-time dependent, with optional ppGpp coupling), and kinetic-constraint targets when USE_KINETICS=True (drives enzyme-catalysed flux toward Vmax·[E]). The DUF report identifies this load-bearing structure as the reason v2ecoli "is best characterised as a hybrid algorithmic simulator, rather than as a numerical integrator of a mathematically defined dynamical system" (Sec. 4.1.1) — the LP is non-autonomous in a way that's tied to the discrete-time loop, and consecutive solutions hop between vertices of different polytopes as enzyme abundances change. Replacing the LP with a kinetic ODE turns the metabolism layer into a real dynamical system with well-defined process likelihoods.

2. Validity — does it actually work?

2.1 Millard ODE reproduces published reference state

Standalone runs of the Millard 2017 SBML via pbg-copasi recover the published BioModels MODEL1505110000 initial state to solver tolerance for all major intermediates (G6P, F6P, FDP, PEP, PYR, ATP/ADP, NADH/NADPH, MAL, AKG). Recovery confirmed 2026-05-24; see millard_published_ref_state.html.

2.2 End-to-end 600 s simulation completes

Replacing the Metabolism Step with MillardPDMPMetabolism in the baseline composite, the WCM simulates 600 seconds of cell time without crashing. Equilibrium, transcription, translation, and replication all continue to fire — central metabolite count changes from the Millard ODE propagate into the structured bulk store through the combined Process's in-line bulk-delta emission.

2.3 Bulk store deltas land correctly

The combined Process resolves 22 species mappings from Millard's mM concentrations to v2ecoli's bulk-store IDs (via v2ecoli/data/millard_v2ecoli_species_map.yaml) and applies integer-rounded count deltas per tick. ATP[c] drifts from v2ecoli's initial ~5.6 M molecules toward Millard's steady-state ~1.5 M molecules over the first ~10 ticks, then tracks the ODE thereafter — confirming the write-path is live.

2.4 Architecture seam check

Three Process edges constitute the PDMP metabolism slot: millard-pdmp-metabolism (ODE + control + bulk emit), fba-bridge (kept on disk for observability, currently unwired — see "wiring quirk" below), and lqr-controller (multi-state Riccati). Removing Metabolism from the step registry frees the LP and its GLPK dependency from the per-tick path entirely; the new path is ODE-only.

3. Performance vs prior baseline

Phase 1's perf story is honest: the PDMP composite is currently slower than the kFBA baseline, because basico/COPASI's ODE integrator costs more per tick than GLPK's LP solve at this problem scale. Phase 4 (committed in this PR) ports the full Millard to JAX/Diffrax and demonstrates the lever exists, with caveats.

The basico ODE cost dominates the current PDMP composite. 7. What lands here that wasn't in the original Tier-A perf pass

This PR pivots the optimisation effort from micro-tuning the existing kFBA path (Tier A in earlier conversation, ~1.05× cumulative gain on the metabolism + mass-listener + initiation hot spots) toward the architectural replacement the DUF report identifies as the right intervention. Tier A is left as incremental in-place wins that can be revisited or reverted as the PDMP substitution matures; nothing in this PR depends on Tier A's edits.

Tier A audit, for context: roughly 60% of the original WCM's wall time was in upstream wholecell wheels or native solvers (polymerize Cython, GLPK simplex), where the v2ecoli-side levers were small. The PDMP move attacks the metabolism path at the model-class level — it removes the LP entirely rather than tuning around it.
